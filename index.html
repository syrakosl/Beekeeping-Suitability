General Info

This interactive web map was created as part of a Diploma Thesis submitted to the National Technical University of Athens (NTUA), School of Rural, Surveying and Geoinformatics Engineering.

The thesis focuses on determining the most suitable areas for beekeeping activities in the Regional Unit of Aitoloakarnania, Greece, using geospatial analysis and multi-criteria evaluation techniques.

Scenario Explanation

Original Scenario:
This scenario represents the standard suitability model where vegetation is prioritized the most (weight: 0.40), reflecting the critical role of floral resources in beekeeping. Slope follows (0.25), as accessibility and terrain affect hive placement and apiary management. Temperature (0.15) ensures a favorable microclimate for bee activity. Road network and proximity to settlements are given equal lesser importance (0.10 each), mainly for logistical support.

Alternative Scenario 1 ‚Äì Temperature Emphasis:
This scenario gives primary importance to temperature (0.45), ideal for identifying areas with optimal climatic conditions for year-round bee activity and hive survival. Vegetation and slope are moderately weighted (0.20 and 0.15), while road network and settlements maintain minimal but equal influence (0.10 each).

Alternative Scenario 2 ‚Äì Accessibility Emphasis:
Focused on accessibility, this scenario heavily weights both proximity to the road network and settlements at 0.35 each. This approach favors areas that can be easily reached for maintenance and honey transport. Vegetation, slope, and temperature are considered secondary, each with a weight of 0.10, ensuring they are still factored into the analysis but with reduced priority.

Conclusions

The multi-criteria evaluation approach applied here demonstrates the importance of integrating environmental and infrastructural parameters to assess the suitability of locations for beekeeping.

Users can visually compare different suitability scenarios and better understand spatial decision-making through this interactive tool.
